GETTING STARTED

The creation, receiving, maintenance, usage, and disposal of records are all part of the records management process. A record is content that documents a commercial transaction in this context. Contracts, memoranda, paper files, electronic files, reports, emails, films, instant message logs, and database records are all examples of documentation. Physical boxes on-site or at a storage facility can be used to keep paper records. Digital records can be kept on local storage media or in the cloud.

BASIC FUNCTIONALITY OF THE SYSTEM

DASHBOARD

A data management platform that enables us to monitor, analyze, and visualize important performance indicators, statistics, and recent transaction data from the past. It comprises staff counts, which indicate the number of employees we have, transaction counts for this month compared to last month, and revenue for monthly profits compared to last month's earnings.


TRANSACTION

Transactions including data collection, storage, retrieval, modification, and any other kind of activity that results in the storage of all previous transactions are included here. A dependable, consistent, and efficient system for processing records. CRUD process is utilized to our system for creating a collection of stored procedures in SQLite is one of the most efficient approaches.

Using the transaction:
Click the "Add Transaction" button to create a new record with a unique ID. Enter the customer's first name and last name, as well as a date, time and location. Transaction table provide an option to edit a new record, however altering its contents will not affect the ID. For removing a record, the option to permanently delete it is also shown on the transaction table.
